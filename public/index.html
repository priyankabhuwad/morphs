Why Face morphing?

Missingkids – Canada’s missing kid resource center, offers families support in finding their missing child. The idea for the project struck when I was sent a statement via mail .The mail consisted of an infant missing from the year 1989. Even 26 years later people still find the motivation to find their kids without even knowing how they may look. This is where face morphing makes its way.

Morphing is a special effect in motion pictures and animations that changes (or morphs) one image or shape into another through a seamless transition. I intended to use the same process in changing ones appearance making them older. This won’t only accomplish its task in finding for the person but will also help the grieving parents to find peace when there is little hope of reconciliation.

Face morphing can also help in grabbing hold of a criminals who have been evading the law for a long period of time and can be used to generate different variations of how the culprit will look if he is under disguise. This will give rise to new ways of crack cold cases down. The procedure would require less manipulation in the face but would also require more possibilities in facial changes of a criminal than grabbing hold of a person who went missing in the 1980’s.

Step 1

To start, select a small brush of size 2 or 3 and set the Range to “Midtones” in the Options bar. You want to make sure the image has not too many highlights, is balanced and shows the middle tines of the image so that it isn’t too gray or flat.
A good place to start is the Eyes. Brush some straight lines for the appearance of wrinkles. Hold the [Alt] key and trace the lines to highlight (Dodge) and add some depth to the wrinkles, similar to what I have done in the image below.

Step 2

Trace the natural lines of the face and extend the lines a little. This will strengthen and lengthen the natural wrinkles. Using the same Burn and Dodge technique, add some “accent” wrinkles with a brush size of 1 or 2.

Step 3

Choose a wider brush size of 4 or 5. Lower the Exposure to around 3-4% and darken the areas around her eyes, cheeks, and some of the wrinkles.

Step 4

Next area to touch up is the Neck. Increase the Exposure, and create some thick white lines vertically on the neck by holding down the [Alt] key. Trace the light lines after letting go of the key.
